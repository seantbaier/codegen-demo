Spain Language Immersion — Barcelona

Location: Barcelona, Spain
Duration: Semester
Cost: $14,500
Credits: 12–16

Program Overview

Immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of Barcelona while advancing your Spanish language skills. This semester-long program combines rigorous academic coursework with hands-on cultural experiences, placing you in the heart of one of Europe's most dynamic cities.

Designed for students at the intermediate level and above, the Spain Language Immersion program offers an unparalleled opportunity to achieve near-fluency through daily interaction with native speakers, university-level classes taught entirely in Spanish, and a carefully curated series of cultural excursions.

Students who complete this program report an average 2-level improvement on the ACTFL proficiency scale and a 94% satisfaction rating.

Program Highlights

  • Enroll directly in courses at the Universitat de Barcelona alongside local students
  • Live with a carefully selected Spanish host family for full cultural immersion
  • Weekly conversation exchange (intercambio) sessions with local university students
  • Guided excursions to Madrid, Seville, and the Costa Brava
  • Intensive orientation week including survival Spanish, city navigation, and cultural workshops
  • Access to on-site resident director and 24/7 emergency support

What Makes This Program Unique

Unlike many study abroad programs that group international students together, our Barcelona immersion places you directly into the local university system. Combined with a host family stay and our language pledge program, you will use Spanish as your primary language from day one. Our small cohort size (maximum 25 students) ensures personalized academic advising and a tight-knit community while still encouraging independence and exploration.

Credit Transfer

Credits earned through the Universitat de Barcelona are fully transferable. Our academic team works directly with your home institution's registrar to ensure seamless credit transfer. A transcript is issued within 6 weeks of program completion.

98% of students successfully transfer all earned credits to their home institution. Pre-approval forms are available during the application process.

Language Requirements

Applicants must have completed at least 4 semesters of college-level Spanish (or equivalent) and demonstrate intermediate proficiency. A language assessment will be administered during the application review process to ensure proper course placement.

Housing Options

Host Family (Recommended)

Live with a carefully vetted Spanish host family in a private room. Host families provide two meals per day (breakfast and dinner) and offer an authentic window into daily Spanish life. All families are located within 30 minutes of the university by public transit.

Shared Student Apartment

Share a furnished apartment in the Eixample or Gracia neighborhoods with 2–3 other program participants. Apartments include a full kitchen, shared living space, and Wi-Fi. A meal stipend of $150/month is provided in lieu of host family meals.

85% of past participants chose the host family option and rated it as the single most impactful element of their immersion experience.

Meals

  • Host Family: Breakfast and dinner daily, plus a weekend lunch
  • Apartment: $150/month meal stipend; students prepare their own meals
  • Lunch is not included — students explore Barcelona's abundant and affordable food scene

Local Culture & Daily Life

Barcelona is a walkable, transit-friendly city with a rich cultural calendar. Students enjoy a T-Jove transit pass (included in program fees), easy access to beaches, world-class museums, and a thriving food scene. The city is safe, welcoming, and endlessly explorable.

  • Public transit pass included (metro, bus, tram)
  • Average student lunch cost: $8–$12
  • Students are encouraged to join local clubs, sports teams, and volunteer organizations
  • Weekend travel to other European cities is easy and affordable via budget airlines and trains

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak Spanish fluently to participate?

▾
No, fluency is not required. However, you should have an intermediate level of Spanish (typically 4 semesters of college-level coursework or equivalent). You will complete a language placement assessment as part of your application so we can ensure you are placed in appropriate courses.

Can I use my existing financial aid for this program?

▾
In most cases, yes. Federal financial aid (grants, loans, work-study) typically applies to approved study abroad programs. Contact your home institution's financial aid office early in the application process to confirm. We also offer program-specific scholarships.

What is the application timeline?

▾
Applications for the fall semester open on January 15 and close on March 31. For the spring semester, applications open August 1 and close October 15. Decisions are communicated within 4 weeks of the deadline. Early applications are encouraged as spots are limited to 25 students.

Will my credits transfer to my home university?

▾
Our program has established credit transfer agreements with over 200 U.S. institutions. During the application process, you will complete a pre-approval form with your academic advisor. Our team also provides direct support to ensure credits are accepted. Historically, 98% of credits are transferred successfully.

What happens in an emergency while I'm abroad?

▾
Your safety is our top priority. The program includes 24/7 emergency support, comprehensive health and travel insurance, and an on-site resident director who lives in Barcelona. We also conduct a thorough safety orientation during your first week, covering local emergency services, the nearest hospital, and our emergency protocols.

Can I travel independently on weekends?

▾
Yes. Students are free to travel on weekends and during program breaks. Barcelona is well connected by budget airlines, trains, and buses to destinations throughout Spain and Europe. We provide a travel planning workshop during orientation and ask only that you register your travel plans with the resident director for safety purposes.
